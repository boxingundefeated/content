* **Material:**  Yoga mats come in various materials, including PVC, rubber, and cork. PVC mats are inexpensive and provide good traction but may have environmental concerns. * **Thickness:**  The thickness of a yoga mat affects how comfortable it is to practice on. Standard yoga mats are typically 3-4mm thick, providing adequate cushioning without compromising stability. Thicker mats (5-6mm) might be more comfortable but could lead to instability during balance poses.

* **Stability:**  Choose a mat that offers enough stability for your practice style. Heavier mats usually provide better support, while lighter mats may not stay in place during dynamic flows.

* **Portability:**  If you plan to travel with your yoga mat, make sure it's lightweight and easy to roll up. Some mats fold in half, making them even more compact for storage or transport.

2. Allow your mat to dry completely before rolling it up.

3. Store your mat away from direct sunlight and extreme temperatures.

4. Avoid sharp objects that could puncture or damage the surface of your mat.
