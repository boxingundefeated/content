### Considerations for Choosing Infuze Water Bottles

When selecting an infuze water bottle, consider the following factors: 

1. Size and capacity: Choose a bottle that meets your daily hydration needs. Common sizes range from 18 oz to 32 oz.

2. Build material: Opt for bottles made of BPA-free materials like Tritan, glass, or stainless steel. Tritan is lightweight and unbreakable, whereas stainless steel is insulated to keep your water cold or hot for longer.

3. Lid type: Look for leak-proof seals and easy-to-handle lids. Some bottles also offer a wide-mouth design for easy cleaning.

4. Infuser design: Pick a design that best suits your fruits, vegetables, or herbs. Some infusers are detachable, while others have a removable fruit infusion strainer.

### General Advice for Using Infuze Water Bottles

Here are some tips to make the most out of your infuze water bottle: 

* Clean the bottle and infuser thoroughly before first use and after each use to maintain freshness and prevent mold growth.

* Experiment with different fruit and herb combinations to discover your favorite flavor infusions. Adding fresh mint leaves or cucumber slices can also enhance the taste and offer additional health benefits.

### How can I increase the shelf life of my infuse water bottle?

To prolong the life of your infuse water bottle, avoid storing it in direct sunlight, extreme temperatures, or damp environments. Using a protective silicone sleeve or carrying case may also help extend the lifespan of your infuse water bottle.
